A "bit" is a single binary digit, a one or a zero. There are two different places in computing where bits get counted. In color displays, and in processing.

The number of bits tells you how many colors can simultaneously be displayed on the screen. For example, "8-bit color" means that you can only have a maximum of 256 colors on your display. Modern systems use "24-bit color", 8 bits each for red, blue, and green intensities, for a total of 16.78 million colors.
